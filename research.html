Research behind watching, subtitles, and self-directed learning

Lingüa Player is not a clinical or classroom intervention, and these studies do not test Lingüa Player directly. They support the broader learning ideas the app is built around: audiovisual input, captions/subtitles, vocabulary attention, and self-directed mobile learning.

Captioned Video and Vocabulary

  • Captions can support listening comprehension.
  • Vocabulary gains are stronger when learners notice words in context.
  • Effects depend on task design and learner level.
Montero Perez, Van Den Noortgate, Desmet · 2013 · System

Audiovisual Input and L2 Learning

  • Real video can contribute to language-learning outcomes.
  • Visual context helps make spoken input easier to follow.
  • Support tools matter when the source material is authentic.
Sutton & Webb · 2026 · Studies in Second Language Acquisition

Subtitles, Captions, and Vocabulary

  • Subtitle language changes where attention goes.
  • Captions can reinforce the spoken form of new words.
  • Flexible subtitle modes fit different learning moments.
Peters, Heynen, Puimege · 2016 · System

Self-Directed Mobile Learning

  • Motivation changes across a self-directed learning routine.
  • Mobile learning benefits from autonomy and small repeatable actions.
  • Review tools help turn watching into a sustainable habit.
Chamani, Razi, Xodabande · 2023 · Discover Education
